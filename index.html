Why this dataset works for demo

The synthetic dataset is designed with an intentionally unfair historical screening pattern: privileged urban men from elite institutes are shortlisted at much higher rates, while equally qualified women, disabled candidates, and applicants from marginalized caste groups are filtered out more often.

What EquiLens shows

The prototype demonstrates the full audit journey: dataset review, subgroup disparity checks, proxy detection, model fairness metrics, mitigation comparison, and a final plain-language report for decision-makers.

Dataset design

Scenario: an automated hiring model ranks applicants for analyst interviews. Historical data encodes structural bias through elite-college preference, urban-location preference, and under-selection of women, disabled candidates, and marginalized caste applicants.

Decision target Shortlist for interview.
Sensitive attributes Gender, caste_group, disability_status, region.

Selection rate by group

Gemini-style explanation

The audit suggests the model is not only learning merit signals such as aptitude score and interview score, but also reproducing historical preference for urban, elite-institute, non-disabled, upper-caste male applicants. Several proxy variables appear to encode structural disadvantage rather than job relevance.

Deployment blocked

Recommendation: remove or down-weight proxy-heavy features, rebalance training data, and use fairness-aware thresholding before deployment.

Mitigation strategies tested

Pre-processing Reweigh underrepresented groups and reduce historical skew.
Feature review Remove direct use of postal cluster and soften institute-tier dependence.
Post-processing Adjust decision threshold to reduce false negatives for protected groups.

Audit summary

Risk level High, because selection outcomes differ sharply across protected groups.
Main cause Historical training labels and proxy-heavy features amplify structural disadvantage.
Action Retrain with fairness constraints and keep human review in the loop.

Suggested next steps

1 Replace historical labels with reviewed labels where feasible.
2 Add fairness monitoring to every model refresh.
3 Publish plain-language audit summaries for compliance and public accountability.
